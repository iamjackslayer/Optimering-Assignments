Assignment Instructions

The Diet Problem

Hand in no later than Tuesday, February 11th.

In this assignment we will solve a real-life version of the diet problem, inspired by the efforts of Las Olsen that made the Danish media in summer of 2014: http://nyhederne.tv2.dk/samfund/2014-08-17-økonom-du-kan-leve-sundt-under-20-kroner-om-dagen. We think that Las Olsen formulated and solved a linear program similar to the diet problem example of the first lecture, but that he did not include your favorite food items in his model (unless you are a big brussel sprout fan). He clearly also left out some obvious constraints (e.g., to eat 500 g of oats one also need to add some milk to the menu).

LP Formulation

See if you can do better by formulating a linear programming model with some of your favorite food items included. Your model should be based on nutritional data taken, e.g., from http://frida.fooddata.dk and prices taken, e.g., from http://www.nemlig.com or based on your personal shopping experiences. Your model should specify a daily diet minimizing total price and satisfying (at least) the following constraints:

  • The total energy content should be at least 10.000 kJ.
  • Macro nutrients should be constrained as recommended in the Nordic Nutrion Recommendation (NNR 2012). This means that between 25% and 40% of the total energy should come from fat, between 45% and 60% of the total energy should come from carbohydrates, and between 10% and 20% of the total energy should come from protein. We use that 1g of fat contains 38 kJ of energy and that 1g of either carbohydrates or protein contains 17 kJ of energy. You can assume that all the energy comes from protein, carbohydrates or fat, unless you add alcohol to the menu. If you do add alcohol, at most 5% of the energy should come from alcohol.
  • You are willing to eat according to the solution for at least a week.

Solving the problem

You should find an optimal solution to your LP formulation using the Python implementation of the Simplex algorithm scipy.optimize.linprog given in the SciPy library.

You want to minimize the price, but maybe, when you see the solution, you will realize that you need to add some constraints to make the menu reasonable. Many more constraints can be found in the NNR 2012, for instance concerning added sugar, salt, and vitamins.

If you don't already have the Python language installed, the Anaconda distribution is recommended.

See LP-example1.py for code that solves the LP example of the first lecture.

What to hand in

Hand in the following:

  1. A description of your model. Describe the meaning of variables and constraints.
  2. A short explanation of how you arrived at your model (including the process of possibly adding additional constraints).
  3. An optimal solution found using scipy.optimize.linprog.

Note

  • You may discuss the assignments with other students, but:
  • You must hand in your own individual solutions.
